**Full voiceover:**
"PET asks a different question from anatomy. A radiotracer is a molecule labeled with an isotope that produces a detectable signal. FDG-PET tracks glucose uptake, which is useful in many cancers but not all. Other tracers target specific receptors or pathways. PET/CT or PET/MRI combines molecular activity with anatomy. In theranostics, PET can also ask whether the treatment target is present before a therapeutic isotope is used. PET is powerful because it sees biology across the body."
