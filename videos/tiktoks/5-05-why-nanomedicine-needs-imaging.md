**Full voiceover:**
"Nanomedicine makes a promise about delivery. But after injection, particles may go to the tumor, liver, spleen, kidney, or inflamed tissue. Imaging tests where they actually went. It can ask how much arrived, how long it stayed, whether the payload was released, and whether biology changed. Without imaging, a failed therapy is hard to interpret. Was the target wrong? Was the payload weak? Did the particle never arrive? Imaging turns delivery from a claim into evidence."
