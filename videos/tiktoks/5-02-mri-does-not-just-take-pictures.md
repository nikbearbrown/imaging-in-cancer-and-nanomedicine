**Full voiceover:**
"Most people think MRI means a detailed picture. In cancer research and nanomedicine, MRI can also be a measurement system. Quantitative MRI asks for numbers: diffusion, perfusion, relaxation parameters, tissue structure, and treatment response signals. A tumor may look the same size while its vascularity, cell density, necrosis, or delivery environment changes. That is why quantitative imaging matters. The future of imaging is not just prettier pictures. It is better biological measurement."
